Microbial ecology · genomics · experimental evolution

Alexander B. Alleman

I study how bacteria evolve, compete, and coexist — using Methylobacterium and the leaf-surface microbiome as a window into microbial ecology.

My lab treats the phyllosphere as a tractable system for questions that span ecology and enzyme mechanism: the metabolic trade-offs a cell makes when it takes up a new pathway, and the eco-evolutionary dynamics that let a synthetic community hold together over time. The work leans on comparative genomics, experimental evolution, and a fair amount of computation, paired with classic microbial physiology at the bench.

Before joining Western in 2025, I was a postdoctoral fellow with Christopher J. Marx at the University of Idaho. I earned my Ph.D. in Molecular Plant Science at Washington State University with John W. Peters, and my B.A. in Biochemistry and Cell Biology here at Western Colorado University — so this is a bit of a homecoming. Outside the lab you'll find me mountain biking, cycling, and running the trails around the Gunnison Valley.
