What the lab works on

Research

How do bacteria adapt to their environment and to one another? We answer that with approaches ranging from wet-lab experimental evolution to comparative genomics.

Experimental evolution & metabolic trade-offs

When a cell acquires a foreign metabolic pathway, integrating it is rarely free. We in lab evolution to watch that integration happen in real time.

Microbial community & eco-evolutionary dynamics

Using barcoded, defined communities, we study how bacterial strains persist, compete, and coexist in differing substrates and stressfull envrioments.

Genomics & phylogenomics of methylotrophs

Comparative and phylogenomic analysis of the Methylobacterium clade — including work reinstating the genus Methylobacterium after 16S rRNA had led the field astray, a cautionary tale about single-marker taxonomy in the genomic era.

Bioenergetics of nitrogen fixation

Metabolic modeling and physiology of how obligate aerobes such as Azotobacter vinelandii balance oxygen and nitrogen reduction, and how diazotrophs generate the low-potential electrons that nitrogenase demands.

Methods. The lab pairs classic microbial physiology at the bench with heavy computation, including: genome assembly, pangenome and phylogenetic pipelines, DNA barcode lineage tracking, and constraint-based metabolic modeling.
